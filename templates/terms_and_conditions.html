1. Nature of RPM Services

Our RPM service is designed to assist healthcare providers in monitoring certain chronic conditions by collecting health data remotely. The purpose of RPM is to help fine-tune medications and potentially reduce some in-person visits to your healthcare provider over time.

However, our RPM program is not an emergency monitoring or diagnostic service. Readings are reviewed:

  • At most once per week
  • As little as once per month

This delay means we do not provide real-time monitoring or urgent medical evaluation. Medicare guidelines for RPM is to check the data a minimum of once per month to fulfill their billing criteria.

2. Scope of Monitoring

The scope of RPM is limited to one or more chronic conditions that are predefined and agreed upon by your care provider. This service does not replace comprehensive care for all your health concerns. RPM is intended to give you better control over specific health parameters through expert oversight and adjustment from home.

Our specialties include:

  • Hypertension
  • Atrial fibrillation
  • Atrial flutter
  • Supraventricular tachycardia
  • Oxygen management in COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease) or other chronic lung disorders
  • Chronic congestive heart failure management
  • Oxygen use in asthma

Note: Diabetes management is something we specialize in but will not currently offer, and may be added in the future.

For atrial and ventricular tachycardia, COPD, and Congestive Heart Failure, please be advised that these conditions can rapidly decompensate. RPM should not be relied upon for emergency management in these cases.

Your readings will be securely stored in our system, and medications may be adjusted periodically based on those trends.

We may offer tiered plans in the future that allow for closer symptom-based monitoring, which could potentially reduce reliance on acute care. These expanded offerings will depend on available resources and reimbursement capabilities over time.

3. Not for Emergency Use

Due to the limited monitoring frequency, our RPM services must not be used as a substitute for emergency or urgent care.

If, for example, you experience any of the following:

  • Blood pressure above 190/110
  • Oxygen saturation below 88%
  • Blood glucose above 400 mg/dL
  • Or any symptoms that concern you

Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. Waiting for our RPM review could result in serious harm or delayed care.
